1 Process Overview

Maps the full C-suite and buying group landscape per account (parent and child entities) leveraging ISC and 3rd-party sources. Produces continuously validated contact records across complete buying groups, identifies whitespace, and alerts on movement of client and former IBM executives in and out of accounts.

Primary Outcome
Continuously validated buying group records per account
Scale
2,200 accounts, 8+ CXO roles per account
Time Savings Target
60 to 80% efficiency gain over current AI tooling (~30-40% accuracy)
Future Reusability
VERY HIGH. Competitive displacement, alumni network, M&A restructuring

3 End to End Process

Closed loop process. Signals from source systems flow through the AI stages and feed back into upstream models.

01

MAP

Ingests ISC relationship, account hierarchies (parent/child), and 3rd-party sources (ZoomInfo, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, D&B) to build a complete buying group org chart per account, identifying all C-suite and key decision-maker roles.

02

VALIDATE

Continuously verifies contact records against external feeds: title accuracy, email deliverability, company affiliation, and reporting structure. Flags stale or conflicting records for resolution.

03

ENRICH

Fills gaps in buying group coverage by identifying missing roles (no CFO contact, incomplete IT buying committee) and sourcing candidate contacts from 3rd-party providers.

04

TRACK

Monitors executive movement: client contacts changing companies, former IBM executives joining target accounts, new C-suite appointments. Generates real-time alerts.

05

SYNC

Pushes validated, enriched buying group back to ISC and Real-Time CDP, maintaining a single source of truth for downstream targeting, personalization, and account-based orchestration.

6 Risks, Dependencies, and KPIs

Open Risks & Dependencies

  • Privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA) constrain what contact info can be stored and how executive movement tracking is implemented (legal review required).
  • 3rd-party data quality varies by region. Coverage gaps may limit buying group completeness in some regions.
  • Entity resolution across parent/child hierarchies is technically complex but well-understood. Matching and dedup logic requires tuning for MCC's account structure.
  • 3rd-party licensing scope must be confirmed (ZoomInfo, LinkedIn, D&B coverage).
  • CDO partnership and seller adoption are critical dependencies.

KPIs

  • CXO record accuracy rate
  • Buying group completeness (% with key roles identified)
  • Automation level (% of validation without manual intervention)
  • Completeness (buying group coverage)
  • Reduction in manual lookup time across CXO team and analysts
